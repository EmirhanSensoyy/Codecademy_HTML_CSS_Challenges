Preface

Muhammad Ali (born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. January 17, 1942 in Louisville Kentucky - June 3, 2016 in Phoenix, Arizona)
was an American professional boxer.
Considered the greatest boxer of all time, Muhammad Ali lost only 5 of his fights in his career.

About

He is of African-American and Irish descent. At the age of 12 he was introduced to boxing and soon entered the amateur ranks at the National AAU and Golden Gloves Championships. He returned to the professional ranks in 1960, winning gold in the heavy lightweight division in Rome. At the age of 18, after winning the gold medal at the Rome Olympics, his fame began to grow. In 1964, at the age of 22, he defeated S. Liston to become World Champion.
After this victory, he announced his conversion to Islam. He took the name Muhammad Ali and had to take a break from boxing which he loved so much, from 1967 to 1970. He retired from boxing as Champion in 1978.

Then in 1984 he developed Parkinson's disease. An Olympic and World Champion, defeated only 5 times in his professional career, Muhammad Ali was the only man to win every title until the age of 36,winning 56 fights, 37 of them by knockout. Muhammad Ali, who had been struggling with Parkinson's disease for a long time br died on 3 June 2016 in the hospital where he was being treated for respiratory illness.
